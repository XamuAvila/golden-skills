## Core Philosophy

The golden rule of management: **do what it takes to get the best out of your team**. Your performance as a manager is measured by the performance of your team. This requires humility — consistently putting the needs of direct reports above your own.

### The Skill Tree Mental Model

Think of your career as an RPG skill tree. Moving from IC to management means starting at Level 1 in an entirely new branch. Technical skills are necessary but insufficient — you need people leadership, architecture thinking, and general decision-making skills.

### Key Frameworks

**Three Stages of Problem-Solving**:
1. **Ingest** — Collect data via docs, 1:1s, active listening. Stop when patterns repeat.
2. **Synthesize** — Step back for a few days. Form hypotheses from different angles.
3. **Act** — Execute a plan, validate results, restart the loop if needed.

**Two Types of Decisions** (Bezos framework):
- **Type 1**: Irreversible — decide methodically, carefully, slowly. Example: choice of programming language.
- **Type 2**: Reversible — decide quickly with high-judgment individuals. Example: button color.
- Most day-to-day technical decisions are Type 2. Using Type 1 process for Type 2 decisions leads to slowness.

**Team Decisioning Models**:
- **Independent**: Manager decides alone → fastest, least buy-in.
- **Straw Man**: Manager drafts, team iterates → medium time, good buy-in.
- **Codevelopment**: Build from scratch together → slow, maximum buy-in.
- Optimize for buy-in: missionaries, not mercenaries.

**Breaking Ties**: Three narratives:
1. "Your way — you made a convincing argument."
2. "My way — I have broader context, here's why."
3. "My way — it's a tie, and I'm accountable for the outcome."

### Servant Leadership

Adopt servant leadership: focus on empowering others, building transparency, communication, collaboration, and growth. Ask: "Which option enables the team to do their best work?"

### The Three Types of Startup CTO

1. **Tech-Focused** (Chief Architect): Forward-thinking strategy, architecture, POCs, fewer direct reports.
2. **People-Focused** (VP of Engineering): Hiring, culture, managing ICs and managers, internal processes.
3. **Externally-Focused** (Head of Technical Sales): Blog posts, conferences, sales support, developer evangelism.

Most founder CTOs start tech-focused. If the company needs a focus that isn't your superpower, delegate it — hire a VPE or developer evangelist accordingly.
